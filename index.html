Che Guevara

Hasta la Victoria Siempre

Guerrillero Heroico by Alberto Korda. Often cited as the world's most famous photo.

Here's a timeline of Che Guevara's life:

  • 1928 (June 14th) - Ernesto Guevara was born in Rosario, Argentina. His parents, Ernesto Guevara Lynch and Celia de la Serna y Llosa were a fairly wealthy middle class couple
  • 1948 - Attended Buenos Aires University where he studied medicine
  • 1950 - Made a 4,500km tour of rural Argentina
  • 1951 (January - July) - Took a year out from study and together with Alberto Granado made an 8,000km tour of much of South America. He was struck by the poverty and poor working conditions that he saw.
  • 1953 (March) - Completed his university education and passed his medical degree
  • 1953 (July) - Set off on another travelling tour of South and Central America taking in Bolivia, Peru, Ecuador, Panama, Costa Rica, Nicaragua, Honduras and El Salvador
    • 1953 (December) - Met survivors of the Moncada attack in Costa Rica
  • 1953 (December 24th) - Arrived in Guatemala where the President, Jacobo Árbenz Guzmán, who had been democratically elected, was trying to reduce poverty by redistributing land
  • 1954 - Decided to settle in Guatemala but was unable to find work as a doctor so he had to take a variety of jobs to earn money. He was becoming increasingly convinced that capitalism was responsible for the extreme poverty of South and Central America and became interested in the theories of Karl Marx
  • 1954 (late Spring) - Became increasingly involved in revolutionary activites in Guatemala
  • 1954 (June) - American forces invaded Guatemala and President Árbenz was forced to resign
  • 1954 (September) - Left Guatemala and went to Mexico. Also, married Hilda Gadea who was pregnant with their child
  • 1955 (July) - Met Raul and Fidel Castro. Joined their revolutionary movement to overthrow Batista's government in Cuba.
  • 1955 (November 25th) - Guevara was one of eighty-two men who left Mexico in the yacht Granma bound for Cuba
  • 1955 (December 2nd) - Reached Cuba but the group was attacked by Batista's forces. Guevara and the Castro brothers were among the few survivors of the attack and fled to the Cuban hills.
  • 1956 (February) - Birth of a daughter, Hilda Beatriz Guevara Gadea, known as Hildita
  • 1956 - Began recruiting Cubans to their revolutionary cause
  • 1958 (Summer) - Batista sent forces into the Cuban hills to wipe out the rebels. However, the Cuban army was no match for the rebels who knew the hills and used guerilla tactics against the Cubans. Many Cuban soldiers deserted and joined the revolutionary army.
  • 1958 (December) - Guevara led a sortie into Santa Clara which successfully overthrew and occupied the city. Bastista fled
  • 1959 (January) - The rebels entered the Cuban capital, Havana and began setting up a new government
  • 1959 (January to June) - Guevara and Raul Castro were given the task of purging the army and police of those that remained loyal to Batista. Most of those tried were found guilty and executed. The trials and executions were condemned worldwide.
  • 1959 (February) - Became a Cuban citizen
  • 1959 (May) - Divorced Hilda Gadea
  • 1959 (June 2nd) - Married Aleida March
  • 1959 (October) - Appointed head of the Department of Industry of the National Institut of Agrarian Reform (INRA) in Castro's new government
  • 1959 (November 26th) - Appointed President of the National Bank of Cuba
  • 1960 - Completed his first book 'Guerrilla Warfare'
  • 1960 (July 26th) - Spoke at the First Latin American Youth Congress where he declared that the Cuban revolution was a Marxist Revolution
  • 1960 (October) - Wrote a history of the Cuban Revolution entitled 'Notes for the studies about Cuba's revolution'
  • 1960 (October 21st) - Left Cuba to make a tour of other socialist countries including, China, Russia, and Czechoslovakia
  • 1961 (February) - Gave up position as President of the Cuban Bank and became Minister for Industry
  • 1961 (April) - Wrote 'Cuba exceptional case or avant-garde in struggle against imperialism'
  • 1962 - Guevara continued to align Cuba with the Soviet Union a move which led to the Cuban Missle Crisis in October
  • 1963 (June) - Sent a revolutionary group to Argentina
  • 1963 (Summer) - Wrote 'Revolutionary war passages'
  • 1963 (December) - Was a speaker at the United Nations General Assembly where he spoke about the link between armed revolution and true socialism
  • 1964 - Travelled the world speaking about socialism and armed revolution
  • 1965 (Spring) - Returned to Cuba where he resigned from government stating that he believed that his true calling was as a revolutionary and determined to help bring about revolutions in other countries
  • 1965 (Summer) - Left Cuba for Africa where he visited a number of countries including the Congo where he hoped to bring about a Communist revolution
  • 1966 (March) - Returned to Cuba to avoid being caught in Africa
  • 1966 (Late March) - Left Cuba and returned to South America travelling through Argentina, Uruguay, Paraguay, Brazil and finally Bolivia
  • 1966 (November) - Joined a Bolivian revolutionary group
  • 1967 (March) - Led successful attack on the Bolivian army
  • 1967 (August) - The Bolivian army destroyed a number of revolutionary groups, yet Che survived
  • 1967 (October 8th) - Bolivian forces attacked remaining rebels. Che was injured and taken prisoner
  • 1967 (October 9th) - Che Guevara was executed. He was shot a total of nine times before he died

"The true revolutionary is guided by a great feeling of love. It is impossible to think of a genuine revolutionary lacking this quality."

- Che Guevara
